Discover Kottayam

The Land of Letters, Lakes, and Lush Landscapes

Welcome to Kottayam

Kottayam, often called the "Akshara Nagari" (City of Letters) for its rich literary heritage, is a scenic district in Kerala, India. Nestled between the Western Ghats and the Vembanad Lake, it's known for its backwaters, rubber plantations, and historic churches.

Top Attractions

Kumarakom – Backwater Paradise

Famous for its houseboat cruises, bird sanctuaries, and serene Vembanad Lake views.

Don't miss: Luxury houseboat stay and Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary

Vaikom – A Spiritual & Historical Hub

Home to the Vaikom Mahadeva Temple, one of Kerala's oldest Shiva temples.

Don't miss: Vaikom Satyagraha Memorial and backwater walks

Ilaveezha Poonchira – A Hidden Hill Station

A panoramic valley surrounded by three hillocks, perfect for trekking.

Don't miss: Sunrise/sunset views and camping

Why Kottayam is Unique?

  • Literary Capital: First 100% literate district in India
  • Rubber Capital: Largest producer of natural rubber in India
  • Church Hub: Known as the "Land of Churches"
  • Backwater Beauty: Peaceful backwater experiences
