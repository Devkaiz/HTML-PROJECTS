Kottayam: The Land of Letters, Lakes, and Lush Landscapes

Introduction to Kottayam

Kottayam, often called the "Akshara Nagari" (City of Letters) for its rich literary heritage, is a scenic district in Kerala, India. Nestled between the Western Ghats and the Vembanad Lake, it’s known for its backwaters, rubber plantations, and historic churches.
Best Time to Visit: October to February (pleasant weather) Nearest Airport: Cochin International Airport (76 km away) Major Languages: Malayalam, English

Top Attractions in Kottayam

  1. Kumarakom – Backwater Paradise

    Why Visit? Famous for its houseboat cruises, bird sanctuaries, and serene Vembanad Lake views.
    Key Activities:
    Stay in a luxury houseboat
    Visit Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary (migratory birds like Siberian storks)
    Enjoy traditional Kerala meals on a canoe

  2. Vaikom – A Spiritual & Historical Hub

    Why Visit? Home to the Vaikom Mahadeva Temple, one of Kerala’s oldest Shiva temples.
    Key Highlights:
    Vaikom Satyagraha Memorial (freedom struggle history)
    Backwater walks & fishing villages

  3. Ilaveezha Poonchira – A Hidden Hill Station

    Why Visit? A panoramic valley surrounded by three hillocks, perfect for trekking.
    Best Experience:
    Sunrise/sunset views
    Camping under the stars

  4. Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple – Architectural Marvel

    Why Visit? A 16th-century temple with stunning murals and traditional Kerala architecture.
    Festival Alert: Annual Kottayam Pooram (colorful elephant procession)

  5. Poonjar Palace – A Glimpse of Royalty

    Why Visit? A historic palace with ancient artifacts, wooden carvings, and a royal chariot.

Why Kottayam is Unique?

  • Literary Capital of Kerala

    First 100% literate district in India.
    Home to Malayala Manorama & Mathrubhumi, leading Malayalam newspapers.

  • Rubber Capital of India

    Largest producer of natural rubber in the country.
    Visit rubber plantations to see latex extraction.

  • Church Hub

    Known as the "Land of Churches" (St. Mary’s Orthodox Church, St. Joseph’s Syro-Malabar Church).

  • Backwater & Eco-Tourism

    Kumarakom & Vembanad Lake offer peaceful backwater experiences (less crowded than Alleppey).
